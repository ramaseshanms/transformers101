The Fragmentation Problem

During generation, KV caches grow. Storing them contiguously is wasteful. Paged Attention breaks them into non-contiguous blocks, just like OS virtual memory.

üìí ELI12: The Notebook Analogy

Imagine writing a very long essay in a notebook.

  • Traditional System: You must leave 50 blank pages perfectly in order just in case your essay gets long. If you only write 1 page, 49 are wasted!
  • Paged Attention: You write on whatever loose leaf of paper you find. You just keep a "Table of Contents" (Block Table) that says: "Page 1 is on desk, Page 2 is on the shelf, Page 3 is under the cat."

No wasted space, and you can stitch the story together perfectly when reading!
